Gold is highly valued for its lustrous yellow appearance, malleability, ductility, and resistance to corrosion and tarnish. Found in both native form and ores, gold has been used for thousands of years in jewelry, currency, and as a symbol of power and wealth.

Gold has been known and valued by humans since prehistoric times, making it one of the first metals to be discovered and used. Its origin lies in stellar nucleosynthesis, where gold atoms were formed during supernova explosions or neutron star collisions, and later dispersed across the universe.

Gold has various applications, including:


Jewelry: Gold is widely used in jewelry, coins, and luxury items due to its beauty, durability, and cultural significance.


Medicine: Gold nanoparticles are being explored for drug delivery and cancer treatment.


Aerospace applications: Gold coatings protect spacecraft and satellites from radiation and heat in space.
