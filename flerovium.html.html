Flerovium is a synthetic, superheavy, and highly radioactive metallic element, first produced in a Russian laboratory by bombarding plutonium with calcium atoms. It exists only in minute, short-lived atoms, making its physical properties and uses unknown, though its chemical properties are theorized to be similar to those of lead. The element is named after the Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions and is of interest in research concerning the "island of stability".

Flerovium has no practical, commercial, or industrial applications becuase it is a synthetic, highly unstable, and extremely radioactive superheavy element, produced in only a few atoms at a time. Its sole purpose is in scientific research to test and refine nuclear theories and models, providing deeper insights into the nature of superheavy elements and the forces within the atomic nucleus.
