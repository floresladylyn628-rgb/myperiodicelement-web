Dubnium is a synthetic, highly radioactive transition metal. Dubnium does not occur naturally and is produced in laboratories through nuclear reactions.

Dubnium was first claimed in 1967 by Soviet scientists at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research in Dubna, Russia, who synthesized it by bombarding americium-243 with neon-22 ions. They proposed the name neilsbohrium(Ns) after the physicist Niels Bohr. In 1970, American scientists at the Lawrence Berkeley Laboratory in California synthesized Dubnium by bombarding californium-249 with nitrogen-15 ions. They proposed the name hahnium(Ha) in honor of Otto Hahn, a German chemist. A dispute emerged between Soviet and American teams over the naming rights. In 1997, the international Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry resolved the conflict by officially naming the element dubnium after the Russian research site in Dubna.

Dubnium has no practical applications due to its extreme radioactivity, short half-life, and the difficulty in producing it.
