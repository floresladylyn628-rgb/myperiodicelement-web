Technetium is a chemical element, symbolized as Tc, and is a silvery-gray, radioactive metal. It's a transition metal with unique properties. Technetium is primarily used in nuclear medicine for diagnostic imaging and research due to its radioactive isotopes. Its applications are limited due to its rarity and radioactivity.

Technetium was the first element to be artificially produced. It was discovered by Carlo Perrier and Emilio Segrè in 1937. They isolated the element from molybdenum samples that had been bombarded with deuterons. Technetium is named after the Greek word "technētos," meaning "artificial," reflecting its synthetic origin. Due to its radioactivity and short half-life, technetium is not found naturally on Earth in significant quantities.

Technetium has various applications, including:


Nuclear medicine: Technetium-99m is widely used for diagnostic imaging in medicine, such as bone scans and cardiac stress tests.


Medical research: Technetium's radioactive properties make it useful for research in medicine and biology.


Industrial applications: Technetium can be used in certain industrial processes, such as corrosion studies.
