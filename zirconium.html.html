Zirconium is a chemical element, symbolized as Zr, and is a transition metal. It's a lustrous, gray-white metal that's resistant to corrosion. Zirconium is used in various applications, including nuclear reactors (for fuel rod cladding), ceramics, medical implants, and specialized alloys. Its unique properties make it useful for high-performance applications.

Initially used in limited applications, zirconium's unique properties have led to its widespread use in nuclear reactors, ceramics, medical implants, and other advanced technologies.

Zirconium has various applications, including:


Nuclear reactors: Zirconium is used for fuel rod cladding due to its neutron transparency and corrosion resistance.


Chemical processing: Zirconium's corrosion resistance makes it useful for equipment in chemical processing industries.


Medical implants: Zirconium is used in medical implants, such as hip and knee replacements, due to its biocompatibility and corrosion resistance.
