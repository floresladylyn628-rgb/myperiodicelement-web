Lead is a dense, soft, and malleable. It is bluish-gray when freshly cut but tarnishes to a a dull gray over time. Known since ancient times.

Lead has been known and used by humans for thousands of years, making it one of the earliest metals to be utilized. Its history and origin are tied to ancient civilizations due to its abundance, ease of extraction, and malleability.

Lead has various applications, including:


Ammunition: Lead is commonly used in bullets and shot due to its density and malleability.


Construction: Lead is used in roofing materials, cable sheating, and weights for balancing equipment.


Alloys: Lead is used in Alloys such as solder, pewter, and bearing metals to improve durability and workability.
