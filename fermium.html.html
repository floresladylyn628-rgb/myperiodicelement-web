Fermium is a synthetic, highly radiactive element, belonging to the actinide series. It was discovered in 1952 in the debris of the first hydrogen bomb test by a team led by Albert Ghiorso. Named after physicist Enrico Fermi, fermium is produced in minute quantities through nuclear reactions and is primariy used for research into heavy elements and nuclear science.

Fermium has very limited applications due to its extreme rarity, high radioactivity, and the difficulty of producing it. Its primary uses are for scientific research and nuclear synthesis.
