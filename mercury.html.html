Mercury is a heavy, silvery liquid metal at room temperature. Known since the ancient times, it is unique among metals for its liquid state under normal conditions. Mercury is highly dense, toxic, and has a high surface tension, which allows it to form spherical droplets.

The element mercury has been known since ancient times and does not have a single discoverer. Early civilazations, such as the Chinese and Eqyptians, were aware of mercury as far back as 1500 BCE. It was primarily obtained from the mineral cinnabar, where it was extracted through heating. The Greeks later named it "hydragyrum", meaning "liquid silver," which is the origin of its modern chemical symbol "Hg".

Mercury has various applications, including:


Electrical applications: Found in mercury switches and relays due to its conductivity and liquid state.


Cultural and decorative uses: Mercury was used in mirrors for its reflective properties in earlier times.


Industrial processes: Mercury is used in the production of chlorine and caustic soda in the chlor-alkali process.
