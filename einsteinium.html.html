Einsteinium is a synthetic, highly radioactive element, belonging to the actinide series. It was discovered in 1952 during the analysis of debris from the first hydrogen bomb explosion. Named after physicist Albert Einstein, einsteinium is produced in extremely small quantities and has no significant practical applications due to its scarcity and intense radioactivity.

Einsteinium has very limited applications due to its extreme radioactivity, scarcity, and short half-life. However, it is primarily used in scientific research.
