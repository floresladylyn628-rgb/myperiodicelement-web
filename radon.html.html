Radon is a radioactive noble gas, formed naturally as a decay product of uranium and thorium. It is colorless , odorless, and highly dense, with significant health risks due to its ability to accumulate indoors and emit harmful alpha radiation, making it a laeding cause of lung cancer in non-smokers.

Radon was discovered in 1899 by Friedrich Ernst Dorn, a German physicist, who observed that a radioactive gas(later named radon) was emitted during the decay od radium. It was initially called "radium emanation" (Ra Em) due to its association with radium, it was later renamed radon in 1923 to align with the naming of noble gases

Radon has various applications, including:


Cancer therapy: Radon was historically used in radiation therapy to treat cancers, though safer alternatives are now preferred.


Geological surveys: Radon is used to detect uranium deposits and study geological activity, such as predicting earthquakes.


Scientific research: Radon isotopes are studied for their role in radioactive decay chains and nuclear physics.
