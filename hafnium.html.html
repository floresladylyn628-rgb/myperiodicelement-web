Hafnium id a silvery-gray, lustrous metal that is highly resistant to corrosion due to the formation of a thin oxide layer on its surface. Hafnium is chemically similar to zirconium, often occurring alongside it in minerals like zircon.

Hafnium was discovered in 1923 by Dirk Coster and George Hevesy, two scientists working in Copenhagen, Denmark. Its name comes from "Hafnia," the Latin name for Copenhagen, honoring the city where it was identified.

Hafnium has various applications, including:


Plasma cutting: Hafnium is used in plasma torches as an electrode material because it withstands high temperatures and provides excellent conductivity.


Ceramics: Hafnium compounds are used in refractory ceramics that can widthstand extreme temperatures, such as furnace linings.


Research: Due to its isotopes and resistance to corrosion, hafnium is used in geochronology to date rocks and minerals.
