Thulium is a soft, silvery-gray rare earth metal with tomic number 69, belonging to the lanthanide series.

Thulium was discovered in 1879 by Swedish chemist Per Teodor Cleve while analyzing the mineral erbium oxide. By carefully removing impurities, Cleve identified two new other components: one he named holmium and the other thulia, which contained thulium. The element was named after Thule

Thulium has various applications, including:


Anti-counterfeiting: Thulium's flourescence under ultraviolet light makes it useful in euro banknotes to detect counterfeit currency, providing a secure anti-fraud measure.


Laser technology: Thulium-doped lasers, such as Tm:YAG lasers, emit infrared light and are highly effective in surgical procedures, including urology and dermatology.


Medical technology: Thulium-170, a radioactive isotope, is widely used in portable X-ray devices for medical imaging, especially in remote areas.
