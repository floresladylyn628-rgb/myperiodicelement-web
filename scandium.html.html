Scandium is a silvery-white transition metal with the atomic number 21 and symbol Sc. It's relatively soft and has a high melting point.

Scandium was discovered by Lars Fredrik Nilson in 1879 in Sweden. He isolated the element from the minerals euxenite and gadolinite. The name "scandium" comes from the Latin word "Scandia," meaning Scandinavia. Scandium was one of the elements predicted by Dmitri Mendeleev, and its discovery helped to validate the periodic table. Initially, scandium was difficult to produce in pure form, but advancements in technology have made it more accessible for various applications

Scandium has various applications, including:


Aerospace industry: Scandium alloys are used in aircraft and spacecraft due to their high strength-to-weight ratio.


Electronics: Scandium is used in the production of semiconductors and other electronic components.


Alloying agent: Scandium is used to enhance the properties of other metals, such as aluminum.
