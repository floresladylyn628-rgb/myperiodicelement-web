    Russian chemist Dmitri Mendeleev is credited in developing the first table of elements in 1869. He created a table of the known elements in ascending order by atomic weight, grouped together by similarities in properties. He also predicted the discovery of other elements, and left a number of empty spaces to account for elements that were yet to be discovered. At about the same time, a German chemist named Julius Lothar Meyer independently arrived at a periodic table that has remarkably similar conclusions.
