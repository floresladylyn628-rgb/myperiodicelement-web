Americium is a synthetic, radioactive element. It was discovered in 1944 by Manhattan Project team led by Glenn T. Seaborg. It belongs to the actinide series and is named after the Americas. Americium is a silvery metal that oxidizes in air and is primarily used in small amounts in smoke detectors.

Americium has various applications, including:


Medical research: Limited use in targeted radiation therapy and radialogical studies.


Smoke detectors: Americium-241 is widely used as a source of alpha particles to ionize air and detect smoke particles.


Industrial gauges: Used in thickness and density measurements for materials like glass, paper, or metal.
