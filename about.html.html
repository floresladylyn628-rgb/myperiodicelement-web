    What are Elements?

    Elements are the building blocks of matter, consisting of atoms with the same number of protons in the atomic nucleus.

    Types of Elements

    There are 118 known elements, classified into metals, nonmetals, and metalloids, each with unique properties and uses.

    Importance of Elements

    Elements are essential for life, technology, and industry, forming the basis of all matter and driving innovation.

    History of the Periodic Table

    The periodic table of elements has a rich history dating back to the early 19th century. In 1869, Dmitri Mendeleev, a Russian chemist, developed the first widely recognized periodic table.

    Mendeleev's table arranged elements by atomic weight and recurring chemical properties. He predicted the existence of undiscovered elements and left gaps in his table for them.

    Over time, the periodic table has undergone significant revisions, including the addition of new elements and the reorganization of existing ones based on atomic number rather than atomic weight.
