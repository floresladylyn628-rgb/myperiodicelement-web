Seaborgium is a synthetic, radioactive transition metal. Named after the chemist Glenn T. Seaborg for his contributions to nuclear chemistry. It was first synthesized in 1974 by both Soviet and American teams, with the Americans at Lawrence Berkeley Laboratory confirming its creation by bombarding californium-249 with oxygen-18 ions. Seaborgium is part of Group 6 in the periodic table, similar to tungsten and molybdenum

Seaborgium has no applications due to its short half-life and instability.
