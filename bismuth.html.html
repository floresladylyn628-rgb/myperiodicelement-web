Bismuth is a brittle, silvery-white metal with a pinkish hue. It is the heaviest naturally occurring stable element and belongs to group 15 of the periodic table. Known since ancient times, bismuth was often confused with lead or tin due to its similar appearance.

The discovery of bismuth as a distinct element is credited to Claude Francois Geoffroy, a French chemist, in 1753. He demonstrated that bismuth was chemically different from lead and tin.

Bismuth has various applications, including:


Stomach medicines : Bismuth compounds, such as bismuth subsalicylate, are used in medications like pepto-bismol for treating indigestion, diarrhea, and upset stomach.


Lead-free substitutes: Bismuth is a non-toxic alternative to lead in plumbing, solders, and ammunition.


Metallurgy: Bismuth is added to certain alloys to improve machinability and casting properties.
