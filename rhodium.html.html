Rhodium is a chemical element, symbolized as Rh, and is a rare, silvery-white transition metal. It's known for its high melting point, reflective properties, and resistance to corrosion. Rhodium is often used in catalytic converters to reduce vehicle emissions, as well as in jewelry and electrical contacts.

Rhodium was discovered by William Hyde Wollaston in 1803. Wollaston, an English chemist, isolated rhodium from platinum ore. The name "rhodium" comes from the Greek word "rhodon," meaning rose, due to the pink color of its salts. Rhodium is a rare and valuable precious metal, often found with other platinum group metals.

Rhodium has various applications, including:


Catalytic converters: Rhodium is used to reduce emissions in vehicle exhaust systems.


Electrical contacts: Rhodium's high melting point and resistance to corrosion make it suitable for electrical contacts.


Jewelry: Rhodium is used to plate white gold and other metals, giving them a reflective, white appearance.
