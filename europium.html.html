Europium is a soft, silvery-white, rare earth metal, belonging to the lanthanide series. Europium is highly reactive, oxidizing quickly in air and water.

Europium was discovered in 1901 by French chemist Eugene-Anatole Demarcay, who identified it as a separate element while studying impurities in samarium. He named it after the continent Europe, making it one of the few elements directly named for a geographic region. Demarcay used advanced spectroscopy techniques to isolate europium, which had distinct spectral lines, confirming it as a new lanthande.

Europium has various applications, including:


Medical imaging: Used in flourescent markers in biological and medical studies.


Glass and ceramics: Europium oxide is used to create infrared-absorbing glass and optical ceramics.


Anti-counterfeiting: Europium-doped materials are used in banknotes, passports, and other secure documents for UV-responsive markings.
