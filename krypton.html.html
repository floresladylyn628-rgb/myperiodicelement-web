Krypton is a noble gas element, symbolized as Kr. It's a colorless, odorless, and tasteless gas. Due to its unreactive nature, krypton is used in various applications, including lighting, such as fluorescent lamps, and insulation. Additionally, krypton is used in lasers and other specialized lighting applications. Its unique properties make it a valuable element in various industries.

Krypton was discovered in 1898 by Scottish chemists William Ramsay and Morris Travers. They discovered it while experimenting with liquid air and isolating other noble gases. The name "krypton" comes from the Greek word "kryptos," meaning "hidden," due to its elusive nature. Since its discovery, krypton has been used in various applications, including lighting and insulation, leveraging its unique properties as a noble gas.

Krypton has various applications, including:


Lighting: Krypton is used in fluorescent lamps, incandescent bulbs, and flash lamps.


Lasers: Krypton is used in krypton lasers, which have applications in medicine, spectroscopy, and other fields.


Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI): Krypton is used in some MRI machines to improve image quality.
