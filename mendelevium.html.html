Mendelevium is a synthetic, radioactive actinide element, named in honor of Dmitri Mendeleev, the creator of the periodic table. It was first synthesized in 1955 by a team lead by Albert Ghiorso, Glenn T. Seaborg, and colleagues at the Lawrence Berkeley Laboratory by bombarding eisteinium-253 with alpha particles
