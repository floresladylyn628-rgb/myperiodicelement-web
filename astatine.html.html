Astatine is a highly radioactive halogen. It is the rarest naturally occurring element on Earth, found in trace amounts as a product of uranium and thorium decay. Astatine exhibits properties of both halogens and metalloids.

Astatine was first synthesized in 1940 by scientists Dale R. Corson, Kenneth Ross MacKenzie, and Emilio Segre at the University of California, Berkeley. They artificially created it by bombarding bismuth-209 with alpha particles in a cyclotron, producing astatine-211. The name astatine comes from the Greek word "astatos," meaning "unstable," reflecting its extreme radioactivity and short half-life.

Astatine has various applications, including:


Cancer treatment: Astatine-211 is used in targeted alpha-particle therapy, delivering precise radiation to destry cancer cells without damagng healthy tissue.


Radioactive tracers: In research, astatine isotopes serve as tracers to study biochemical processes due to their radioactive properties.


Scientific study: Astatine is used to investigate halogen behavior, providing insights into periodic table trends and heavy-element chemistry.
