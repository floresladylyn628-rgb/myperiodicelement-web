Livermorium is a synthetic, extremely radioactive element. Created in laboratories through nuclear experiments, it lacks natural occurrence and commercial uses, with its primary application being scientific research. Named after the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, it is known for having several highly unstable isotopes, with the longest-lived isotope having a half-life of about 80 milliseconds.

Livermorium has no commercial applications and its only uses is in scientific research, where its creation and properties help scientists understand the behavior and limits of superheavy atoms and nuclear stability.
