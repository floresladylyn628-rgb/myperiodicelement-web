Ruthenium is a chemical element, symbolized as Ru, and is a rare, silvery-white transition metal. It's known for its hardness, brittleness, and high melting point. Ruthenium is often used as an alloying element to enhance strength and durability, and has applications in electrical contacts, wear-resistant parts, and catalysts.

Ruthenium was discovered by Karl Ernst Claus in 1844. Claus, a Russian chemist, isolated ruthenium from platinum ore residues. The name "ruthenium" comes from the Latin word "Ruthenia," meaning Russia, in honor of Claus's homeland. Ruthenium is a rare element and has limited applications due to its scarcity and difficulty in processing.

Ruthenium has various applications, including:


Electrical contacts: Ruthenium is used in electrical contacts and switches due to its high melting point and resistance to wear and corrosion.


Wear-resistant parts: Ruthenium's hardness and wear resistance make it suitable for applications such as wear-resistant coatings and parts.


Catalysts: Ruthenium is used as a catalyst in certain chemical reactions, including those in the petroleum and chemical industries.
