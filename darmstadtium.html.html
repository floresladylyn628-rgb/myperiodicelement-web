Darmstadtium is a synthetic, superheavy element. It is highly unstable and exists only for a few milliseconds before decaying into lighter elements. Being part of the transition metals in in Group 10, its chemical properties are predicted to be similar to platinum and palladium.

Darmstadtium was first synthesized on November 9, 1994, by a team of scientists at the Gesellschaft fur Schwerionenforschung (GSI) Helmholtz Centre for Heavy Ion Research in Darmstadt, Germany. The team, led by Peter Armbruster and Sigurd Hofmann, bombarded a lead target with nickel ions to produce four atoms of darmstadtium-269.The discovery was officially recognized by the International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry in 2003.

Darmstadtium has no practical applications due to its extreme instability and short half-life which is measured in milliseconds to seconds. Its primary use is in scientific research, particularly in studying the properties of superheavy elements and advancing our understanding of nuclear physics.
