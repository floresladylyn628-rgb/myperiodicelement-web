Sodium is a soft, silvery-white alkali metal with the atomic number 11 and symbol Na. It's highly reactive and readily loses one electron to form a positive ion. Sodium is essential for various biological processes and is commonly found in table salt (sodium chloride) and other minerals. Its high reactivity makes it useful in various industrial applications, such as in the manufacture of soap, paper, and textiles.

Sodium was first isolated by Sir Humpry Davy in 1807 through the electrolysis of caustic soda (sodium hydroxide). Davy named the element "sodium" after the Latin word "soda", referring to the plant ashes used to obtain sodium, such as salt (sodium chloride), have been used for centuries, but the element itself wasn't isolated until Davy's experiment. Sodium's discovery paved the way for further research into alkali metals and their properties.

Sodium has various applications, including:


Manufacturing of soap and detergents: Sodium hydroxide (caustic soda) is used in soap making and as a cleaning detergent.


Paper and textile industry: Sodium hydroxide is used in paper bleaching and textile processing.


Lighting: Sodium vapor lamps are used for street lighting and other applications.
