Iron is a silvery-white, ductile, and malleable metal with the atomic number 26 and symbol Fe. It's one of the most abundant elements on Earth and a crucial component of steel, playing a vital role in construction, transportation, and various industrial applications. Iron is also essential for biological processes, particularly in the formation of hemoglobin in red blood cells.

Iron has been known and used by humans for thousands of years, with its discovery dating back to ancient civilizations. The earliest known use of iron dates to around 1200 BCE, during the Iron Age. The Hittites in Anatolia (modern-day Turkey) are believed to have been among the first to smelt and forge iron.

Iron has various applications, including:


Medical: Iron is essential for human health, particularly in the formation of hemoglobin in red blood cells.


Machinery: Iron is used in engine blocks, gears, and other machinery components.


Transportation: Iron and steel are used in vehicles, ships, and trains.
