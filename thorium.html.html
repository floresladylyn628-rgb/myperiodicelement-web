Thorium is a silvery, radioactive actinide metal. It is highly abundant in the Earth's crust and is known for its potential as an alternative nuclear fuel due to its ability to undergo nuclear reactions when converted into fissile uranium-233. Thorium is relatively stable in air, has a high melting point, and is primarily found in minerals like monazite.

Thorium was discovered in 1828 by the Swedish chemist Jons Jakob Berzelius, who named it after the Norse god of thunder, Thor. Berzelius identified the element while analyzing a mineral sample sent to him from Norway.

Thorium has various applications, including:


Gas mantles: Historically used in gas mantles for bright, long-lasting light production.


Alloys: Added to magnesium and other metals to improve strength and high-temperature performance.


Radiation shielding: Utilized in some industrial applications for its ability to absorb radiation.
