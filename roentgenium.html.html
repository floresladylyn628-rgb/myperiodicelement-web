Roentgenium is a synthetic, superheavy element. First synthesized in 1994 by a team of scientists at the GSI Helmholtz Center for Heavy Ion Research in Darmstadt, Germany. Named after Wilhelm Rontgen, the discoverer of x-rays, it belongs to Group 11 of the periodic table, alongside gold, silver, and copper. Due to its extremely short half-life, its chemical and physical properties remain mostly theoritical, though it is predicted to behave similarly to gold.

Roentgenium has no practical applications due to its extreme instability and short half-life. Its primary use is in scientific research, particularly in studying the properties of superheavy elements, nuclear reactions, and the limits of the periodic table.
