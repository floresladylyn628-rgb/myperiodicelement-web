Curium is a synthetic, radioactive element. It was discovered in 1994 by Glenn T. Seaborg and his team during the Manhattan Project. It is named after Marie and Pierre Curie, pioneers in radioactivity research. Curium is an actinide metal that emits alpha particles.

Curium has various applications, including:


Spectroscopy: Helps in studying the properties of materials under neutron borbardment.


Neutron sources: Curium isotopes like Cm-244 and Cm-248 are used as neutron emitters in scientific research and material testing.


Space exploration: Curium has been studied as a potential heat source for thermoelectric generators in spacecraft.
