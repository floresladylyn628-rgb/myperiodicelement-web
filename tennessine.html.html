Tennessine is a synthetic, superheavy element. It was first synthesized in 2010 by a collaboration of Russian and American scientists at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research in Dubna, Russia. Named after the U.S state of Tennessee, it belongs to Group 17 of the periodic table, the halogens, alongside flourine, chlorine, bromine, iodine, and astatine. Due to its short half-life, its properties remain largely theoritical, but it is expected to behave similarly to astatine.

Tennessine has no practical applications due to its extreme instability and very short half-life. Its primary use is in scientific research.
