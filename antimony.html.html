Antimony is a chemical element, symbolized as Sb, and is a brittle, silvery-white metalloid. It's used in various applications, including flame retardants, alloys (e.g., lead-acid batteries), and semiconductors, due to its unique properties. Antimony is often found in stibnite ore (Sb2S3) and has been used by humans for thousands of years.

Antimony has been known and used by humans for thousands of years, with evidence of its use dating back to ancient civilizations in Egypt, Babylon, and Greece. The name "antimony" is derived from the Greek words "anti" and "monos", meaning "not alone", or from the Latin word "stibium", which refers to the mineral stibnite (Sb2S3).

Antimony has various applications, including:


Flame retardants: Antimony compounds are used to reduce flammability in plastics, textiles, and other materials.


Lead-acid batteries:Antimony is used in lead-acid batteries to increase their durability and performance.
