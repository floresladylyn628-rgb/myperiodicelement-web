Californium is a synthetic, highly radiactive element. It was discovered in 1950 by a team of scientists led by Glenn T. Seaborg at the University of California, Berkeley. Named after the state of California, californium is notable for its ability to emit neutrons.

The discovery of californium was officially announced on March 17, 1950. They bombarded curium-242 with alpha particles to produce californium-245, which decayed into californium-244.

Californium has various applications, including:


Oil exploration: Used in neutron logging to identify layers of oil and water in wells.


Medical research: Californium-252 has been studied for cancer treatment using neutron therapy, especially for treating certain inoperable tumors.


Scientific research: Helps in synthesizing heavier elements, such as oganesson, and in studying nuclear reactions.
