Hassium is a synthetic, highly radioactive and extremely unstable superheavy metal. It was discovered in 1984 by German scientists and named after the German state of Hesse. It exists only in miniscule, laboratory-produced quantities, meaning its exact physical apperance is unknown, though it is predicted to be a solid, and its chemical properties are theoritically based on similarities to osmium. Due to its fleeting half-life, hassium has no known practical applications and is used primarily for scientific research into nuclear physics and experimental techniques.
