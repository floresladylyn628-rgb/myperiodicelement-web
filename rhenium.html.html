Rhenium is a silver-white, dense, and corrosion-resistant element. Rhenium is one the rarest elements in Earth's crust and is typically obtained as a byproduct of molybdenumand copper mining.

Rhenium was discovered in 1925 by German chemists Ida Noddack, Walter Noddack, and Otto Berg. They identified the element while analyzing platinum ores and columbite using X-ray spectroscopy. The name rhenium comes from the Latin word "Rhenus", referring to the "Rhine River", near whre the researchers worked.

Rhenium has various applications, including:


Turbines: Used in gas turbines for power generation due to its durability under extreme heat.


Radiotherapy: Rhenium isotopes are used in cancer treatment for radiotherapy and imaging.


Electrical contacts: Used in filaments, thermocouples, and electrical contacts due to its high melting point and conductivity.
